Why choose "End Grain" Cutting Boards?

The end grain is a lot harder than the face grain of wood, usually by around 1.5x times, this creates a much better hardwearing surface to use. Although the end grain may be harder than face grain it has a lesser blunting effect on knife edges. This is due to the oriantation of the wood fibers, the fibers open up around the blade and can close up with minimal damage to the wood making the cut almost invisible.

Looking after your Cutting Board

Each board is sanded smooth and then treated with food grade mineral oil which protects and extends the life of cutting board; for extra protection and durability the board also receives a special blend of natural beeswax and oil mix to protect the board from liquids.

The cutting board should be treated monthly to help keep it in the best condition. Mineral oil is the best choice for cutting boards as it will not go rancid over time unlike many other oils. Mineral oil should be applied on all sides to the board as just using it on one face could cause warping.

To keep your cutting board in the best condition I recommend a few things:
Do not leave the board wet for long periods of time.
Do not put the board in the dishwasher or leave to soak.
Care should be taken in the re-oiling of the board monthly.
(See how to oil your board)

Oiling your Cutting Board

First wipe the board clean, then pour mineral oil (sometimes called butcher block oil) on to the bottom of the board. Using a cloth and moving in a circular motion spread the oil over the base. You will notice that the oil soaks in and it may be necessary to apply multiple coats over a couple of hours. Repeat for both the top and sides of the board.

To further the care of your board you can use a mixture of mineral oil and natural beeswax together (4:1) to create a glossy water proof finish. The mixture can then be applied to the board with a cloth in a circular motion to get it into the pours of the wood. Once applied leave overnight, any excess can be wiped off after.
